What Are Financial Markets?

Understanding the fundamental systems where assets are traded and how they've evolved.

Financial Markets Definition

Financial markets are organized systems or platforms where participants buy and sell financial assets, such as stocks, bonds, currencies, commodities, and derivatives.

Market Types

Markets can be physical, like the trading floors of the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE), or electronic, such as online platforms like Robinhood or E*TRADE.

Historical Context

Historically, markets date back to ancient civilizations: the Babylonians traded grain contracts around 1750 BCE, and European stock exchanges emerged in the 16th century.
