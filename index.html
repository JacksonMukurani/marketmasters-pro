Forex Market Branches

Spot, Forward, Futures, Options, and Swaps - understanding each instrument's unique characteristics and use cases.

Spot Forex Market

This is the most familiar branch for retail traders, where currencies are traded for immediate delivery at the current market price. When you buy EUR/USD in a spot transaction, you're essentially exchanging one currency for another at the prevailing exchange rate.

Forward Contracts

These are customized agreements between two parties to exchange currencies at a predetermined price on a specific future date. Unlike spot transactions, forwards are traded over-the-counter (OTC) and are not standardized.

Futures Contracts

Similar to forwards but standardized and traded on regulated exchanges like the Chicago Mercantile Exchange (CME). Currency futures have fixed contract sizes, expiration dates, and centralized clearing.

Options Contracts

These provide the right, but not the obligation, to buy (call option) or sell (put option) a currency pair at a specific price (strike price) on or before a certain date.
