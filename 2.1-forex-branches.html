Forex Market Branches: Spot, Forward, Futures, Options, Swaps

10 min read Intermediate Updated: January 2024

The Forex market encompasses several distinct but interconnected branches, each serving different purposes and catering to various trading objectives. Understanding these branches is essential because they represent different ways to gain exposure to currency movements.

Spot Forex Market

This is the most familiar branch for retail traders, where currencies are traded for immediate delivery at the current market price. When you buy EUR/USD in a spot transaction, you're essentially exchanging one currency for another at the prevailing exchange rate.

Key Characteristics:

  • Settlement: T+2 (trade date plus two business days)
  • Liquidity: Very high
  • Price Measurement: Pips (0.0001 for most pairs)
  • Trading Hours: 24/5
